## What is Chain of Custody (CoC)?

The chain of custody is a forensic link that documents the chronological handling of physical or electronic evidence. It records the sequence of who had possession, how evidence was transferred, and when it was collected or analysed.

## Importance of Chain of Custody

**Maintaining integrity:**  
Prevents evidence from being contaminated or altered. If contaminated, evidence may be challenged in court and deemed inadmissible.

**To the examiner:**  
Aids in identifying where evidence might be, who created it, and what equipment was used. Allows examiners to model and verify evidence properties.

**To the court:**  
Ensures no weak links exist in the chain ‚Äî proving evidence integrity and investigator credibility.

## How We Established the Chain of Custody

1. Saved original OVA files and created **Golden Copies** (bit-for-bit clones) for all analysis work.
2. Took photos of physical evidence and screenshots of digital evidence.
3. Documented timestamps for all evidence handling.
4. Generated **SHA-256 hash values** using HashMyFiles to verify copy integrity.

## Considerations

- **Work only on Golden Copies** ‚Äî if a blunder occurs on the original, forensic soundness is compromised. Hash values confirm copies are exact replicas.
- **Collecting media must be clean** ‚Äî infected media could corrupt evidence or compromise the examination machine.
